    About

    About Us

                 Carpentry has been our family business. We have been doing it for many generations. Yashvant Raghunath Machkar inherited these skills from his father. He always wanted to make every piece perfectly, no matter how small or big it was. The customers were satisfied with his expertiseHe had the contracts from renowned people like Nandu Paranjape and Balu Phadke. During that time, all work was hand-made. There were no machines to ease the work. The circular saw came much later, and it was still a rarity. Santosh, son of Yashvant Machkar observed the machine closely and then made this machine on his own. This made wood cutting work easy.
                 Santosh was helping his father since his school days. The returns in this business not as much of today, so his father wished that he should start another business or get a job. Santosh started a cycle shop. He had to paint the cycles in his shop. This led him to start business of spray painting various vehicles. During this time the fashion of various colour sticker number plates came (popularly known as Radium number plates). The stickers were not cut by computerised machine that time, so Santosh used to cut stickers by hand. Still, these businesses never paid good money, so he returned to his traditional business of carpentry.
                 Helekar Sweet Mart was his first contract executed with his brother Rajendra. The first successful contract gave him a booster of confidence and he started his own business in a rented shop near Malnaka. He also invested in various wood cutting machines and his business flourished since then. Santosh always had an aesthetic sense since childhood. He used to make various decorative items like Makhar for Satyanarayan Pooja. Initially, those Makhars were made of steel wires and then a banana stem was incorporated in it to make different designs. Santosh used card paper instead of banana stem to make it more durable and attractive. Originally, these were made for the family and friends, but later when the demand increased, Santosh started renting these Makhar for various Pooja. People loved these rotating Makhars and the business boomed.

                 In Konkan, Ganesh Festival is the biggest festival. People not only decorate the Ganesh idol, but also make various mythical and epic-related scenes in life size puppets. Santosh started making such automated puppets. These were a hit among people. When Konkan railway was announced in 1993, he made a moving train model with Ganesh Idol at the centre. The model contained Railway station, tunnel, signal and a bridge. School kids and parents truly appreciated this model. Santosh then shifted to Ghanekar Ali in another rented shop. In 2014, he started his own factory at MIDC and started making furniture there.
                 His son Rohit completed a course in interior design from Mumbai and joined his father in the business. Now, the business was becoming more modern. We started a Kitchen Trolly panelling and readymade furniture business. The new machinery and concepts were welcomed and the new page was turned. Rohit also provided start-to-end solutions for the interior work and designed modular kitchens. Santosh firmly believes that more people from the family are closely involved in the business, it becomes more stable.It it truly said that, the daughters are a reflection of the father.
                 His daughter Trupti has been also helping him in the furniture making process since her college days. Trupti is already becoming known for her acute sense of artistry. The new machinery provided precision, and that gave a boost to the work. Rohit also makes models for the science exhibitions as a hobby. A friend requested him to make a model of the truck. Santosh made this truck not only a scaled down model, but also installed headlights and indicators in the model. This became a sensation in Ratnagiri and many people are ordering similar models from him. This led us to the new era of digital marketing. Our all new products and services will be available on www.cozyliving.com. Customers can browse, enquire and order these products and services from this site. We firmly believe that the online presence will help us in the emerging digital market.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long will it take for the project to complete ?

    Typically, any residential interior project may take between 2 to 4 months of execution time after a detailed plan has been finalized. And a commercial project may take 1.5 to 3 months. Delays can happen if there are changes in design midway or change in specs. Therefore, it is better to take extra time in finalizing details than be in a hurry to start execution.

    Can we get help in procurement of materials?

    We always help you in procurement of materials at reasonable prices. We have a procurement team that works closely with the designers to help cost-effective procurement of any material.

    How much of my personal time required to invest?

    When you hire COSY LIVING, there is no need to personally supervise anything. However, you should ensure that all finishes and materials are run past you or selected jointly. You should have clarity in what exactly you will get at the end. So, your time can be optimized thru systematic, planned material selection.
